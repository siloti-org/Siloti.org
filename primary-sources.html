PRIMARY MATERIALS

Siloti's over 250 transcriptions and arrangments form the core residue of Siloti's musical art. These, alongside program booklets, articles, interviews, and letters are preserved in archives in US and Russia. The following collection curates an inventory to these sources and provides links to digital materials where avaiable.

SCORES

link Barber 2003 Collection

The Alexander Siloti collection: editions, transcriptions, and arrangements for piano solo.

A collection of typeset Siloti editions providing a window into Siloti's work to "create and recreate a vast literature" (Barber 2003, 5). The introduction contains a high-level reflection on the artistic and pianistic impact of Siloti's redesign choices.

Barber, Charles. The Alexander Siloti collection: editions, transcriptions, and arrangements for piano solo. United States: C. Fischer, 2003.

WRITINGS

link Siloti 1923 Virtuoso

Alexander Siloti: Leaves From a Virtuoso's Note Book

Siloti's 1923 article in the Etude magazine, subtitled: "Practical Study Ideas from Personal Contact with Liszt and Rubinstein."

Siloti, Alexander. "Leaves from a Virtuoso's Notebook." Etude. XLI (August, 1923).

link Siloti 1923 Artistic

Many Roads to Artistic Playing: Alexander Siloti

A reflection on the process of developing artistry at the piano by Siloti for the Etude magazine, 1923. Topics Discussed: Learning by teaching, Analyzing one's own playing, The importance of the playing ideal, Development of the musical imagination, Reminiscences about Liszt as conductor.

Siloti, Alexander. "Many Roads to Artistic Playing." Etude. (March, 1923).

My Memories of Liszt: English Translation from the Russian, 1911
